Mission & Engineering Methodology

As a founding member and Avionics Lead, I am responsible for the end-to-end development of the electrical and control systems for a hybrid-electric VTOL aircraft. My approach centers on four key engineering pillars:

  • Architecture & Systems Thinking: Defined the complete avionics topology, specifying interfaces between Pixhawk 6C, onboard compute, and power subsystems to ensure redundancy and fault tolerance.
  • Flight Control (GNC): Configured PX4 firmware mixing for VTOL and fixed-wing modes. Performed PID gain tuning and implemented fail-safe logic for autonomous recovery.
  • Hardware Implementation: Designed wiring harnesses, sensor mounts, and electrical isolation strategies to mitigate high-current EMI affecting avionics.
  • Testing & Validation: Built a V-model testing pipeline using Hardware-in-the-Loop (HIL), PX4 logs, and bench-level component diagnostics.
