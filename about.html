About

Systems · Flight · Steady Work

I like working where hardware, software, and physics meet. I enjoy building systems that keep working when conditions are not ideal and when the data is not clean. That is the part of engineering that interests me the most.

I am comfortable spending long stretches of time alone with a problem, testing ideas and throwing away what does not hold up. I pay attention to how things fail, not only when they look good in a slide. I prefer clear thinking and simple structures over complicated tricks.

Most of my work so far has been around flight systems, sensing, and control. I like projects where you can measure the result in a clear way: the aircraft flies stable, the estimator holds, the data matches the model. That is the type of work I want to keep doing.

I try to carry the same mindset into every project: keep the scope clear, test the thing in front of you, and stay calm when it breaks.

How I like to work

  • • Start simple, then add complexity only when needed.
  • • Write things down so I can see gaps in my own thinking.
  • • Test early, even if the setup is rough.
  • • Treat failures as data, not as a reason to stop.

What I am aiming for

  • • Work on flight systems, guidance, and control in real vehicles.
  • • Build tools and infrastructure that other engineers can rely on.
  • • Stay close to the hardware and the data, not only the slides.
  • • Keep improving my judgment under pressure and uncertainty.
