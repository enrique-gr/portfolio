About

Systems · Engineering · Steady Work

I am a Mechanical Engineering student with minors in Aerospace Engineering and Mechatronics. Through hands on projects and coursework focused on mechanical design, sensing, and system behavior, I have spent most of my time working on real problems where hardware, software, and physical constraints all affect performance.

I try to be useful by owning a clear part of the system and making sure it behaves as expected. That means reading logs, tightening interfaces, writing simple tools, and keeping test plans current. I care about matching the model to the data and to what the hardware is actually doing instead of only focusing on slides.

For an internship I am looking to join teams that build and test demanding systems. I want to support senior engineers by taking real tasks, closing out issues, and improving the pieces I am responsible for. The goal is to be dependable, learn fast, and contribute to work that moves projects forward in a visible way.

I aim to be the kind of engineer who takes ownership, keeps the work grounded in data, and helps the team move faster, not slower.

How I like to work

  • • Start with a clear and simple approach, then add detail when the data shows it is needed.
  • • Write things down so gaps, assumptions, and open questions are easy to see.
  • • Test early, even with basic setups, to find issues before they become hard to fix.
  • • Treat failures as information and track them until the root cause is understood.

What I am aiming for

  • • Take on internship roles where I can support real test campaigns and system builds.
  • • Build tools and small frameworks that other engineers can rely on every day.
  • • Stay close to hardware and telemetry so my work is tied to real behavior, not only theory.
  • • Grow into an engineer who can stay calm under pressure and make clear decisions with limited time.
