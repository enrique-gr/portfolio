Engineering Cycle

Design, Build, Fly (DBF) requires balancing high-performance aerodynamics with structural durability. My work focuses on unifying simulation results with physical manufacturing constraints:

  • Aerodynamics (Theory + Analysis): Performed trade studies on NACA airfoils using XFLR5, including 3D wing stability and induced drag modeling.
  • Structures (FEA + Loads): Conducted ANSYS Static Structural analysis to size carbon fiber wing spars and ribs under 2.5g maneuver loads.
  • Mechanisms (Hardware): Built servo-driven payload release mechanisms via CAD prototyping and mechanical iteration.
  • Flight Testing (Telemetry-Based): Analyzed real flight telemetry to validate aerodynamic predictions and refine stability margins.
